Marcel Dassault

Marcel Dassault was a French aeronautical engineer and industrialist, best known as the founder of Dassault Aviation. He was born Marcel Bloch in Paris on January 22, 1892, to a Jewish family. After serving as a fighter pilot during World War I, he became interested in aviation and started his own company in 1919. His early designs for aircraft were successful, and he soon became a major figure in the French aviation industry.

During World War II, Dassault was imprisoned by the Nazis for his Jewish heritage. After the war, he changed his name to Marcel Dassault and continued to work on aircraft designs. In 1946, he founded the company that would become Dassault Aviation. Under his leadership, the company developed a range of successful military aircraft, including the Mirage III and the Rafale. Dassault was awarded the Grand Cross of the Legion of Honour, France's highest civilian honor, in 1980.

Marcel Dassault passed away on April 18, 1986. His legacy continues to live on through Dassault Aviation, which is still a major player in the global aviation industry.
